Knitting Nebraska Hot Socks

Barbara McCall

Using 11" circular needles

Materials: (Will complete one pair of socks to fit a women wearing size 10M shoe)
1½ skeins Bartlettyarn 2 ply wool (4 oz./210 yds. per skein)
1 spool wooly nylon thread to reinforce foot to prevent stretching
double point needles - size 6 or size needed to obtain gauge
1 set 11" circular needles - size 6
3 stitch markers (two of one color)

Gauge: 4.5 to 5 stitches per inch in stockinette stitch

Directions:
Using 11" circular needles (or double points if you prefer), cast on 39 stitches. Place marker for beginning of round and join. Work in K2 P1 ribbing for 7 inches (40 rows approximately) or desired length. On last row increase 1 stitch to 40 stitches.

Knit 5 rounds.

Divide for heel in the next row by knitting the first 30 stitches after the marker. Using a double point needle, join the wooly nylon and using the wooly nylon along with the wool, knit the 10 stitches before the marker and the 10 stitches after the marker. You will have 20 stitches on one double pointed needle and 20 stitches for the instep remaining on the circular needle. The stitch marker should be right in the middle of the 20 stitches on the double point needle - these are the heel stitches.

Using two double point needles, knit back and forth on the 20 heel stitches until a total of eighteen rows are completed. The last row will be a purl row. If you like, slip the first stitch of each row. Do not discontinue the wooly nylon. I reinforce the entire foot to minimize stretching as the sock is worn.

Turn Heel:
Row 1: K 12, SSK, k1, turn
Row 2: Sl 1, p to 2 sts past marker, p2tog, p1, turn
Row 3: Sl 1, k to 3 sts past marker, SSK, k1, turn
Row 4: Sl 1, p to 3 sts past marker, p2tog, p1, turn
Row 5: Sl 1, k to 4 sts past marker, SSK, k1, turn
Row 6: Sl 1, p to 4 sts past marker, p2tog, p1, turn
Row 7: Sl 1, k to 5 sts past marker, SSK, k1, turn
Row 8: Sl 1, p to 5 sts past marker, p2tog (12 stitches remain)
Row 9: Knit

Pick up stitches for heel gusset as follows using 3 or 4 double point needles for this round:

After row 9 of the heel, with right side facing, use a crochet hook to pick up 12 stitches along the side of the heel flap, placing these 12 stitches on the same needle as the 12 heel stitches. Place a stitch marker (Gusset marker). Using double point needles, knit the 20 instep stitches. Place a stitch marker (Gusset marker). Pick up another 12 heel stitches from the other side of the heel flap. Then knit first 6 stitches of the heel, ending at the original marker. You should have a total of 56 stitches. Work one round even. I prefer to change back to my 11" circular needle from this point to the toe.

Gusset:
Decrease round: Knit to 3 stitches from the gusset marker, k2tog, k1. Knit to the next gusset marker. K1, SSK and work to the end of the round.

Knit one round even.

Alternate the decrease round with a knit even round until you are back down to 40 stitches. You will have 8 decrease rounds. Remove the two gusset stitch markers at this point if you wish BUT do not remove the stitch marker for the beginning of the round. Continue the wooly nylon through the entire foot.

Option:
If the recipient has a wide foot, I work only 7 decrease rounds so that I have 42 stitches for the foot. For a narrow foot, work 9 decrease rounds, leaving 38 stitches for the foot.

Foot:
Knit in rounds until the foot measures 1.5 inches less that the desired length . I usually knit 20-24 rows. In the last foot row, place a stitch marker (toe marker) after stitch 10 and again after stitch 30. If you have altered the number of stitches to accommodate a wide or narrow foot, decrease 2 stitches in the last foot row so that the number of stitches on your needles is a multiple of 4. Caution: if you have less than 40 stitches, your stitch markers for the toe decreases will be placed after stitch 9 and after stitch 27 or 1/4 of the way around and 3/4 of the way around.

Toe:
Knit to last 3 stitches before the first toe marker. K2tog, k1, slip marker, k1, SSK, knit to last 3 stitches before the second toe marker. K2tog, k1, slip marker, k1, SSK, knit to end of row. You should have decreased 4 stitches on this round.

Knit one round even. If you are working on circular needles, change to the double points when necessary.

Continue to alternate decrease rows with knit rows until 8 stitches remain. Reposition these stitches if necessary so that the first four stitches in the row are on one needle and the last four stitches are on another needle. Graft toe using the Kitchener stitch. This decrease sequence makes a pointy-type toe with a vertical graft rather than a horizontal graft. Of course, if you prefer a different toe shaping - use it!

Personal Note:
I tend to knit very tight. I have problems with ladders in my knitting when I use double point needles. To remedy this problem, I use 11" circular needles whenever possible. When I must use double points, I constantly change the break point between the needles to avoid ladders. For example, I would knit to the end of needle 1 and then knit one, two or more stitches from needle 2 onto needle 1. Then I knit the remaining stitches to needle 2. At the end of needle 2, I again knit one, two or more stitches from needle 3 onto needle 2. Round and round, I keep changing the last stitch on each needle. This process works best when I use 5 needles, not 4.

Super Hot Socks:
Use Bartlettyarn 3 ply wool and this same pattern. I use size 8 needles for the leg and size 7 or 8 needles for the foot (size 8 for a man or a wide foot). Skip the reinforcing thread - no wooly nylon - as this is a very heavy sock. It takes one skein of the 3-ply yarn (145 yards) per sock.

These socks are great to wear as slippers. These Super Hot Socks are too heavy to wear with shoes, but might be OK under outdoor insulated boots.

Bartlettyarn becomes softer when washed and holds up great. I also enjoy dealing with the company when I call to order my yarn. They give free yarn color samples. 207-683-2251 Let me know if this pattern works for you. bmccall@inetnebr.com
